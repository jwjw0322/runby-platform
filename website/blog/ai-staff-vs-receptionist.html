AI Staff vs. Hiring a Receptionist: The Real Cost Comparison for Service Businesses

Author RunBy Team
Published March 2026
Read Time 8 minutes

Every day, we hear the same thing from service business owners: "Hiring a receptionist is cheaper than paying for AI automation."

The math seems simple at first: a part-time receptionist might cost $25-30k per year, and that's obviously cheaper than running an AI system, right?

Wrong. And it's not even close.

When you account for salary, benefits, taxes, equipment, training, turnover, and the hidden productivity costs of a part-time employee, the real cost of hiring a receptionist skyrockets. We're going to break down the actual numbers so you can make an informed decision.

The True Cost of a Receptionist: Hidden Line Items Nobody Talks About

Most service business owners only think about salary when evaluating hiring costs. That's a massive blind spot.

Here's what a typical receptionist actually costs:

  • Base Salary: $25,000 - $35,000/year for part-time (20-30 hrs/week)
  • Payroll Taxes (FICA, FUTA, SUTA): ~15% of gross = $3,750 - $5,250/year
  • Health Insurance (if you offer it): $4,000 - $8,000/year
  • Workers Compensation Insurance: $800 - $1,500/year
  • Recruiting & Hiring Costs: Job postings, background checks, interviews = $1,500 - $3,000
  • Training & Onboarding: Your time + materials = $2,000 - $4,000
  • Office Equipment (desk, computer, phone, supplies): $1,500 - $2,500
  • Turnover Cost (annual): 60% turnover = rehiring costs above = $3,000 - $6,000

Total annual cost: $41,550 - $65,750 per year for ONE part-time receptionist.

And that's before you account for:

  • Paid time off (vacation, sick days)
  • Productivity gaps while training new hires
  • The cost of handling customer calls that go unanswered because your receptionist is on break, sick, or dealing with personal issues
  • Service quality inconsistency (different people handling calls = different customer experience)
  • The burden of managing, scheduling, and covering shifts when they don't show up

What About Better Availability? What If You Go Full-Time?

Some owners think, "What if I just hire a full-time receptionist to get better coverage?"

A full-time receptionist ($40,000 - $55,000 salary) would cost:

  • Base salary: $40,000 - $55,000
  • Payroll taxes: ~$6,000 - $8,250
  • Health insurance: $8,000 - $12,000
  • Workers comp: $1,200 - $2,000
  • All other costs above: ~$7,000 - $10,000

Total: $62,200 - $87,250 per year — and you still only get 40 hours a week (8 hours a day) of coverage.

RunBy gives you better coverage (24/7), zero employee management, and costs $3,588 - $7,188 per year. Not even a comparison.

What If You Need Both? (You Don't, But...)

Some larger service businesses run both — a receptionist for in-person duties and an answering service for after-hours calls.

That's a third option, and it would cost you the full receptionist cost PLUS $200-400/month for an answering service. So you're looking at $50,000+ annually for less capability than RunBy alone provides.
