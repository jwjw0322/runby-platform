# RunBy Sales Objection Playbook
## 100 Objections with World-Class Rebuttals

> **How to use this:** These objections are grouped by category. When a prospect raises a concern, identify the category and use the matching rebuttal. Rebuttals use the **Feel → Felt → Found** or **Acknowledge → Reframe → Bridge** framework. Always empathize first, never argue, and end on a question to keep the conversation moving.

## UNIVERSAL REBUTTAL FRAMEWORKS

When you encounter an objection not listed above, use one of these frameworks:

### Feel → Felt → Found
"I understand how you feel. A lot of our clients felt the same way. What they found was [positive outcome]."

### Acknowledge → Reframe → Bridge
"That's a fair point. Here's another way to look at it: [reframe]. And actually, that's one of the reasons [bridge to benefit]."

### Isolate → Solve → Close
"If we could solve [their objection], would everything else we talked about make sense for your business? Great — here's how we handle that: [solution]."

### The Takeaway
When someone is resistant, reduce pressure by pulling back:
"You know what? This might not be the right fit for you, and that's totally fine. But just out of curiosity — what would need to be true for something like this to make sense for your business?"

## TONE RULES FOR OBJECTION HANDLING

1. **Never argue** — agree first, then redirect
2. **Never repeat the same rebuttal twice** — if the first one didn't land, try a different angle
3. **One gentle nudge max** — if they say no twice, respect it and leave the door open
4. **Always end on a question** — keep the conversation going, don't lecture
5. **Use their words** — mirror their language back to them to build rapport
6. **Silence is power** — after delivering a strong point, pause and let them think
7. **Stay curious, not defensive** — "Can I ask what specifically concerns you about that?" beats "But our product is great!"
8. **Name the elephant** — if you sense hesitation, call it out: "It sounds like something is holding you back. What is it? I'd rather address it now."
